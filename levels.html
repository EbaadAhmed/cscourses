Levels of database:

External level:

This level is the userâ€™s view of the database. This level describes that part of the database which is relevant to each user.

Conceptual view:

The community view of the database. This level describes what data has to be stored in the database and in what structure and what are the relationship that exits among the data.

Internal level:

The representation of the data structure that will be used to physically store the database on the computer is said to be internal level. This level describes how the data is stored in the database.

Physical level:

The physical level operates at the lowest level of abstraction, describing the way the data are saved on the storage media such as disks or tapes.
